# DO: Retention Beats Acquisition Every Time

> A leaky bucket can't be filled. Fix churn before scaling growth.

**20 founders** weighed in on this. **22 total mentions** across 20 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Gam Mubes** — lemlist (SaaS, $30M ARR)
> "when you're doing like a software as a service business the name of the game is your retention meaning how long do people stay when they start paying your service"

**Ian Meers** — Oceans (agency, $15M/yr)
> "If you send an email to someone and they get back to you in 5 minutes that feels amazing. If you have a problem and you get on the phone with somebody and they're like you know what I totally understand that's our bad let me comp you a month of service because this is not the experience I want you to have."

**Tibo** — Multiple SaaS Portfolio (SaaS, $700K/mo)
> "Repeat until they cannot live without your software. If you go broad with low retention, you will spend energy pushing people and 99% will flow away after trying."

**Jeremy** — Van Man (e-commerce, $5M/yr)
> "we've always strived for is like customer service like if you have any problem at all we're going to try to make it go away 100% like immediately"

**Ericos** — Kaching Bundles (and Kaching suite of apps) (SaaS, $4.5M/yr)
> "We offer full refunds no question asked because I think the risk of getting one-star review is just too big."

**Andre Heckle Jr** — List Kit (SaaS, $200K/mo)
> "Every single customer that purchases list kit we offer them to get on a one-on-one onboarding call when I talk to a customer I simply ask why did you sign up for this what were you hoping to use this to achieve."

## At $50K-100K/mo

**Alex Lieberman** — Story ARB (agency, $84K/mo)
> "The easiest part of this business is getting customers I think the hardest part of this business is keeping customers."

**Lara Costa** — Cleo (SaaS, $60K/month)
> "We were going on VIP white glove onboarding calls where we literally took people through the product so they could use it correctly because the reason why most people stop using software is because they don't understand it"

## At $10K-50K/mo

**Joe** — Weightley (mobile app, $40K/month)
> "Anytime that someone calls our support line someone answers immediately they're always surprised that someone picked up right away to answer their call."

**Devon** — Supergrow (SaaS, $19,000/month)
> "We were really obsessed with our LTD customers we used to spend like one or two hours every day on intercom answering their question helping them through content creation."

**Nick** — Block to Pin (SaaS, $16K/mo)
> "Treat him as your employer review everything he did ask for the feedback if he has any suggestions implemented on the same day and your job is to make sure he's 100% satisfied."

**Fernando** — Resume Maker Online and AI Carousels Pro (SaaS, $15K/mo)
> "My strategy was to start with carousels but expand and offer other tools so people might come for the carousels and they stay for the whole package."

**Dennis** — Yataphone (SaaS, $14K/month)
> "I used to text every single paying customer for 6 months asking for feedback. This is timeconsuming and you don't always have the energy as a founder for it but this is immensely helpful because it helps you figure out what are user segments and who your app is serving the best."

**Sam** — Algrow (SaaS, $14K/mo)
> "Discord is 10 times better in terms of building a relationship with your users I think young people will go on Discord more than they check their emails"

**Vikash** — Bulk Mockup (SaaS, $12K/mo)
> "We treat customer support as an education channel. Whenever a customer reaches out to us we directly ask them send us your file we will record a custom tutorial or custom loom video for you."

**Abhishek** — Euform (SaaS, $11K/mo)
> "It is all about how good your product is and your customer support which will not only keep your existing customers but they will also refer others to your product"

## At Unknown
