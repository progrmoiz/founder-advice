# DO: Plan Your Escape from Employment

> Build the side project while employed, but have a clear exit number.

**14 founders** weighed in on this. **21 total mentions** across 14 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Ryan Chen** — Neuro (e-commerce, $10 million/month)
> "We knew that we had some type of base and some type of adoption rate right so when we first left our jobs it felt very real when we no longer had a stable paycheck"

**Dicky Bush** — Ship 30 for 30 (online course, $10M/year)
> "If the current vehicle you're in is not leading to more income or more skill if you doubled your effort find something else"

**Greg Eisenberg** — Late Checkout (holding company, $10M+/year)
> "Instead of having one company do $100,000 a month in Revenue you now have companies maybe they're only doing $25,000 a month but if one struggles you now have that built-in insurance."

**Anton** — Letterly (mobile app, $250K/mo)
> "When you see that there is attraction that you see some revenue just go for it leave your jobs and focus 100% on that one"

**Carl Hughes** — Draft.dev (agency, $2.5M/yr)
> "I was able to do it part-time and kind of get myself like make sure it was viable before I went full on into it I think that would have been really tough to put the pressure on myself to like go 100% fulltime."

**Pat Walls** — Starter Story (content site, $1.8M/year)
> "I built my business while I had a full-time job by waking up early going to the Starbucks down the street locking in putting my headphones on putting my playlist on blocking all Sites blocking all distractions turning my phone off and just doing deep work"

**Brett** — Design Joy (agency, $1.3M/year)
> "When I left my job to take on design Joy full-time it doubled the next month so like I wasted so much time like double efforts like keeping my main job"

**Nico** — Neuroframes (SaaS, $100K/mo)
> "you would think as a bootstrap company you are in disadvantage to VC back companies but I think this is often times actually not true. once you have some traction as a bootstrap business you're actually much more free to make moves."

## At $50K-100K/mo

**Alex** — Finn Versus (affiliate marketing website, $1M/year)
> "The aha moment for me in terms of unlocking my side hustling was I need a true 9 to 5 I need to be able to close my work laptop at 5 so that I can side hustle"

**Dawson** — Nifty (web app, $1M ARR)
> "One of the pros is you get to take it where you want you don't have to ask permission and you get to ship to users directly any team is going to slow you down a bit on that."

## At $10K-50K/mo

**Angus Chang** — Bank Statement Converter (SaaS, $40K/mo)
> "make sure you save enough money to survive multiple years of no income if you only have enough money for a few months of runway you're probably not going to make it"

**Ben Boz** — Tech Lockdown (SaaS, $15K/mo)
> "Don't go full-time on your project until you do this - make sure that you have a way to get customers. The customer acquisition problem is a thing you have to solve before you leave your job."

**Sebastian Ro** — Habit Kit (mobile app, $15,000/month)
> "Save money for Sabbatical to focus on development of your app business like I did i saved some money and I just quit my job and set myself a deadline for 12 months"

**Sherman** — Sherman's YouTube Channel (content site, $125,000/year)
> "This is the time to be high risk. In five years if I'm married and have kids I won't be able to take a massive risk like you can't just quit your job because you have to pay for your mortgage."
