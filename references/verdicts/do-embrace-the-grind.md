# DO: Embrace the Grind

> Success requires doing unglamorous work. Learn fast, adapt faster.

**102 founders** weighed in on this. **185 total mentions** across 102 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Ryan Chen** — Neuro (e-commerce, $10 million/month)
> "Success is defined by how high you bounce back from Rock Bottom"

**Saba Kenned** — V.io (SaaS, $40M/yr)
> "If other people have done it why are they so special why can't we do it"

**Gam Mubes** — lemlist (SaaS, $30M ARR)
> "you got to be patient with the results but you got to be impatient with your action put yourself out there do as much thing as possible don't be afraid to document never give up believe in yourself"

**Casey Holiday** — Qalo (e-commerce, $30M)
> "We are so concerned with other people's perception of us and our product and what we're doing that it paralyzes us"

**Ian Meers** — Oceans (agency, $15M/yr)
> "Don't approach decision making in a way that is from fear of choosing the wrong thing just take on New Opportunities because you always are able to maneuver into what you want to do."

**Lane Wagner** — boot.dev (SaaS, $1M/mo)
> "The biggest mistake is not taking action early not starting a side project and the second biggest mistake I think is just focusing on kind of general content consumption about business and not digging into something really hard and specific."

**Colin** — Sheets & Giggles (e-commerce, $1M/month)
> "My best advice I can give people is do you think someone's going to be a good Mentor say Hey you have an hour a week that I can maybe spend with you and buy you lunch every week and ask you questions"

**Aaron Spivak** — Hush Blankets (e-commerce, $48M in 4 years)
> "What the internet afforded us was guys like me are able to go and compete with the big guys because the internet previously you needed to go and knock doors of these retailers and beg for them to take your product"

## At $50K-100K/mo

**Alex Lieberman** — Story ARB (agency, $84K/mo)
> "Everyone talks about taking the leap of faith in entrepreneurship and I just think it's completely a misnomer it's more about like stepping Pebble to Pebble carefully across the river versus jumping over the river."

**Jacob** — Faceless Video (SaaS, $1M ARR)
> "Stay bootstrapped cuz staying bootstrapped forces you to build lean and and really focus on your value if your idea is valuable to others then you can scale healthfully and if it's not then you can move on and adapt quickly"

**Alex** — Finn Versus (affiliate marketing website, $1M/year)
> "You can try and engineer the outcome as much as possible from the gecko but the reality is that you can't see around very many corners and you're going to have to Pivot a bunch"

**Matt** — Swim University (content site, $1M/year)
> "think about lifestyle over business I don't think about money as the outcome cuz it's like there's a million things I could have done to make way more money than I'm making now and that would have been like oh I would have had a team of people and it would have been a nightmare to manage and I would have hated my life so I constantly think about whether or not the decision I'm about to make will have a negative effect on just my the life that I want which is essentially super freaking easy"

**Dawson** — Nifty (web app, $1M ARR)
> "Don't have as much anxiety don't have as much worry cuz it will all work out you know like look at me now it turned out pretty well."

**Mark Lou** — ShipFast (SaaS, $80K/mo)
> "I remove expectations from anything that I would launch I told my wife like hey you know maybe we'll make 100 bucks that would be nice"

**Mark** — None (agency, $80K/mo)
> "People usually underestimate the amount of work you actually need to put in. I always ask myself what's the number of times you need to repeat this specific task that the chances of you not getting a client or a lead will be close to zero."

**Marcos** — The Birdhouse (agency, $70K/mo)
> "Having kind of a mindset shift from tactic hunting to actually implementing so tactic hunting would be you're trying to plan out what am I going to write the implementing is actually going out and doing it and doing it not just once not just for a week but for 6 months 12 months straight"
