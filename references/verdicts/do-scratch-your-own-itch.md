# DO: Scratch Your Own Itch

> Build something you personally need. You become the ideal user.

**15 founders** weighed in on this. **17 total mentions** across 15 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**John Rush** — Unicorn Platform (SaaS, $3M/yr)
> "I used the same process over and over again for all my 20 plus products. It all starts with my own pain at work that I want to solve and then I look for solutions for that pain."

**Sebastian Georgu** — None (SaaS, $250K/mo)
> "I predicted that if I had just built my SAS business on the back of my personal brand and me telling my own fans to buy it it wouldn't survive in the wild when I say wild I mean in the market and among the competition of other SAS companies that would build similar products as mine."

**David Brusser** — FormulaBot (formerly XL FormulaBot) (SaaS, $226,000/mo)
> "People always say scratch your own itch right solve your own problem and my problem was I was getting so bogg down with all of these Junior adal they come knocking on my office door like hey can you help me with this for me"

**Joseph** — Stealth GPT (SaaS, $190K/mo)
> "You need to honestly be able to critique your idea and and be honest with yourself to say is this actually good will people actually buy this or am I just kind of hyping myself up"

**Henry and Dylan** — Clip.co (agency, $2M/yr)
> "We started with our own itch we needed a podcast to get edited so we hired editors and then oh turns out other people have podcasts that they need edited so they just used our editors."

**Dustin** — Magi AI (SaaS, almost $100,000 a month)
> "I knew that people were completely burnt out on subscriptions and seeing all these different AI companies pop up I went to myself people are going to need like five or six different subscriptions just to get the best of the best AI things what if I could solve that one problem for them"

**Nico** — Neuroframes (SaaS, $100K/mo)
> "I believe very strongly in the around find out principle. So meaning just do many random things all across the board. Imagine this as regions in the whole universe of ideas that you populate. Then something magical happens actually when multiple of these things that you've done in the past combine."

## At $50K-100K/mo

**Dawson** — Nifty (web app, $1M ARR)
> "I love waiting until the ideas are really clear and so sometimes I'll take the first two or 3 weeks of a month-long hackathon just letting the ideas bounce around in my brain so that when I do hit the computer I have tons of conviction."

## At $10K-50K/mo

**Julian Nom** — NotForms (SaaS, $37K/mo)
> "The easiest way to find ideas is try to solve your own problems if you're your own user then it's definitely easier to build a great product"

**Samuel Rondo** — Artimis, Story Short, Capacity (SaaS, $35K/mo)
> "Here are my four keys filter number one I will use it myself number two I can see that it already works and three they are not spending thousand on marketing meaning there is a true demand and four the product is simple enough to maintain"

**Mal Baron** — Prayer Lock (mobile app, $21K/mo)
> "Do not waste time trying to build the app of your dreams at first. Instead improve an already successful app. Add your personality. Copy others distribution strategies."

**Sebastian Ro** — Habit Kit (mobile app, $15,000/month)
> "I just build apps that solve my own problems I will always be my first customer and so I always know exactly what's needed in the product what are the requirements and what works and what not"

**Jack Ficks** — Curiosity Quench and PostBridge (mobile app and SaaS, $10,000/month combined)
> "I think the best way to find any good idea is to solve your own problems"

## At Unknown

**Anya** — Rooted (mobile app, over $1M total)
> "If you are passionate about solving a problem then really launching with just an MVP to get that feedback out there. I was just like obsessed with getting the idea out there and I really didn't then listen to the naysayers as much because I was like I need to prove this out myself."

**Steph France** — Gold Mining Framework (framework/methodology, None)
> "The human brain is not wired to come up with a good business idea. Ego bias and overthinking it often gets in the way. So what if you could outsource the whole process to AI?"
