# DON'T: Give Up Too Early

> Most founders quit right before it clicks. Give it 12-24 months minimum.

**14 founders** weighed in on this. **18 total mentions** across 14 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Lane Wagner** — boot.dev (SaaS, $1M/mo)
> "Think about your business education as a content consumption phase and then an action phase um and I think you know through traditional education we're kind of taught to think this way right i go to college for four years and then I do stuff i think that's absolutely the wrong way to approach business."

**Julian** — Gravel (mobile app, $440K/mo)
> "Keep pushing then don't give up too soon. It's a long road. Building it is just not even half of it. Be ready to do all the hustling."

**David Park** — Jenny AI (SaaS, $3M/yr)
> "If you really care about your dream and if you really care about making something that is used by millions of people people then you can't give it like a onewe shot you can't you can't even give it a year shot you have to really put your all into it sacrifice everything for many many years."

**Charlie Chang** — Charlie Chang YouTube Channels + Paired Staffing (content site, $200-250K/mo)
> "With enough persistence with enough risk-taking you can just pretty much do anything you want"

**Pat Walls** — Starter Story (content site, $1.8M/year)
> "Don't give up on the first try either keep trying and eventually you'll get your first customer"

## At $50K-100K/mo

**Mark Lou** — ShipFast (SaaS, $80K/mo)
> "I failed 30 startups around that but if you don't quit then at some point you'll win it's just a matter of not quitting before you win"

## At $10K-50K/mo

**Loic** — Drop Magic (SaaS, $45K/mo)
> "Don't get emotionally attached to a project just use simple deadlines and milestone as the only source of truth to carry on or not. I've wasted so many years to just believing in something that had no traction just because I was emotionally attached to the logo or the project or the team members."

**Angus Chang** — Bank Statement Converter (SaaS, $40K/mo)
> "those first two years of hard work you don't get paid much but you kind of get the money for that hard work in like years four and onwards"

**Maddox Schmidtoffer** — DuckMath (content site, $15K/mo)
> "The biggest thing was staying consistent. This would have never came to be for me if I'd stopped any of those four years that I had the site"

**Chris** — Wish List (mobile app, $150K/year)
> "If I had set my goal 6 years ago to have 150k a year I would have thrown away that project. So my first goal was not to get the million dollars but to have a random person who I don't know download the app."

**Demitro** — Screenshot One (SaaS, $12K/mo)
> "You need to be persistent enough to try anything to just get to this first dollar."

**Ivan** — Lancer (SaaS, $10K/month)
> "If I could give myself from a young from few years ago any advice it would be to actually start building software products right there and then. I delayed jumping into what I call arena to optimize for income and financial security."

## At Unknown

**Lots** — Multiple apps (Bible Buddy, Magic Music, Toxic Traits, Pray Screen) (mobile app, $500,000 total from 4 app sales)

**Kevin Espiritu** — Epic Gardening (content site, tens of millions a year)
