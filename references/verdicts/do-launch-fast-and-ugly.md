# DO: Launch Fast and Ugly

> Ship the MVP in weeks, not months. Perfection is the enemy of traction.

**61 founders** weighed in on this. **75 total mentions** across 61 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Ryan Chen** — Neuro (e-commerce, $10 million/month)
> "The truth is nothing will ever be perfect so you just have to start if you don't start then all the dreams you have in your head will have a 0% chance of coming to life"

**Gam Mubes** — lemlist (SaaS, $30M ARR)
> "our first MVP was just two weeks of work I closed basically the first 100 customers doing live demos and outbound"

**Colin** — Sheets & Giggles (e-commerce, $1M/month)
> "If you want to do $100,000 campaign you need 30% of your total on day one. You need 10,000 emails cuz an email list reasonably converts at about 3%"

**Oliver Brocato** — Tabs Chocolate (e-commerce, $11M/yr)
> "I think that like honestly you could probably start anything like any sort of ecom brand at least with an MVP with like 10 grand."

**Dicky Bush** — Ship 30 for 30 (online course, $10M/year)
> "If you're spending time thinking you have to have the perfect note taking system and Publishing Cadence and content calendar and editing system before you start writing that's the first thing to avoid just hit publish"

**Yaser** — Chatbase (SaaS, $6.8M ARR)
> "If I don't do this someone else will so I released chatbase to my 16 followers"

**Julian** — Gravel (mobile app, $440K/mo)
> "I'm a massive supporter of Reddit as a distribution channel. I posted this thread on how I built Gravel and shared the technical specs around it."

**Jeremy** — Van Man (e-commerce, $5M/yr)
