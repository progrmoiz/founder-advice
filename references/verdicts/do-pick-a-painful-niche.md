# DO: Pick a Painful Niche

> Narrow markets with real pain pay better than broad ones with mild annoyance.

**63 founders** weighed in on this. **82 total mentions** across 63 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Ian Meers** — Oceans (agency, $15M/yr)
> "Creating a small vertical in which you can succeed is really key. You have to stand out and one of the best ways to stand out is to be the best in your category."

**Lane Wagner** — boot.dev (SaaS, $1M/mo)
> "The most dangerous thing you can do is try to serve multiple customer personas."

**Colin** — Sheets & Giggles (e-commerce, $1M/month)
> "If people ask themselves what am I best in the world at it's a great place to start for like what business they should start"

**Greg Eisenberg** — Late Checkout (holding company, $10M+/year)
> "We Believe community-based products outperform non-community based products so we build businesses based on the ACP funnel."

**Blake Anderson** — Riz GPT, Umax, CI (mobile app, $10M total across 3 apps)
> "When you find a niche that's heavily monetized with physical products that's a good indicator that the niche converts it's also a good indicator that you can probably build a software or application to solve a problem within it"

**Ericos** — Kaching Bundles (and Kaching suite of apps) (SaaS, $4.5M/yr)
> "When I was thinking of what do I want to build in the Shopify app marketplace I already had three main criteria. First one was it has to be pretty easy to build. The second criteria was pretty broad market. The first criteria was I needed low competition."

**Anton** — Letterly (mobile app, $250K/mo)
> "For majority of people it's better to find something that is already working and that can be done better"

**John Rush** — Unicorn Platform (SaaS, $3M/yr)
> "The most important thing when you build a startup is the idea and the founder idea fit. People should build for their own work, they should solve their own problems."
