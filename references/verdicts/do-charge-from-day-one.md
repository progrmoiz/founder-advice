# DO: Charge Real Money From Day One

> Free users give you noise. Paying customers give you signal.

**51 founders** weighed in on this. **64 total mentions** across 51 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Saba Kenned** — V.io (SaaS, $40M/yr)
> "Everything we would like nickel and dime every single purchase to just keep operating expenses as low as possible"

**Gam Mubes** — lemlist (SaaS, $30M ARR)
> "if you can tie any of your software or product or service to the revenue people are going to make your product becomes associated with success"

**Lane Wagner** — boot.dev (SaaS, $1M/mo)
> "Let people really understand the product before we ask them to pay for it."

**Oliver Brocato** — Tabs Chocolate (e-commerce, $11M/yr)
> "People that make money I'm talking real money they don't make it off the cash flow they don't make it off the yearly sales they're making it off the exit with Drop Shipping there is no brand equity."

**Dicky Bush** — Ship 30 for 30 (online course, $10M/year)
> "The fastest way to monetize is actually build the skill yourself and then borrow someone else's audience and go write for them"

**Greg Eisenberg** — Late Checkout (holding company, $10M+/year)
> "Instead of charging $1,500 a month which most agency owners do we charge on average $1.5 million a year why because if we come up with a whole new vision for where to take your company often times those products generate 9 to 10 figures of Revenue."

**Blake Anderson** — Riz GPT, Umax, CI (mobile app, $10M total across 3 apps)
> "I try to go with lower price points for a couple of reasons one I want as many people as possible to be able to use my applications people are more likely to tell their friends about it get generally more positive user sentiment on social media but then it also is more sustainable for long-term growth"

**Tim Stoddard** — Stazzi Internet Marketing (agency, $5.2-5.5M/yr)
> "I still think it's the best business for somebody to start because you just need a client once you get your first client all of a sudden you have a profitable business."

## At $50K-100K/mo

**Dawson** — Nifty (web app, $1M ARR)
> "I got those signups by optimizing this page for the call to actions to sign up for the email both in the header and in just a massive button above the fold using drop shadows and borders properly to draw the user's eyes towards these buttons."

**Mark and Julia** — Papermark (SaaS, $75K/mo)
> "You need to turn that excitement around open source into product momentum so convert them into paying customers or even users of your tool only then you can actually make it a successful business."

**Alex Garcia** — Marketing Exam (and 4 other newsletters) (content site, $65K-70K/mo)
> "Think of your newsletter as Facebook ads as Instagram has as Tick Tock ads where you have the reach you have the potential for a return on ad spend so now how do I get people to want to pay money to get in front of that group"

**Lara Costa** — Cleo (SaaS, $60K/month)

**Thomas** — Packager (SaaS, $60K/month)

**Ure** — Rummer, Parakeet AI, Optivase, Parakeet AI Apply Agent (multiple (hardware device, SaaS, B2B SaaS, AI agent), $60K/mo)

**Adam Liddell** — Portfolio of mobile apps (mobile app, $50-60K/mo)

**Scott** — None (productized service, $50K/mo)

## At $10K-50K/mo

**Sajila Mazafir (CJ)** — Code Guide (SaaS, $42K/mo)
> "We're not doing any trials because we're using top-of-the-line AI models and AI models right now are not cheap"

**Dominico** — Multiple apps (Pitch 2.0, OneUp.ai, YourCoverLetter.com, RecapAI.com, QuitsBot.io, WebDesigner.io, SoftGen.ai) (SaaS, $500K ARR)
> "The most important thing buyers want to know are ARR gross margins average revenue per user lifetime value customer acquisition cost churn rate."

**Steven Creada** — Puff Count (mobile app, $40K/mo)
> "When I changed puff count to a hard pay wall and I made users commit to a free trial before they could use any features in the app it changed my business overnight my conversion rate shot through the roof upwards of 20 25%."

**Julian Nom** — NotForms (SaaS, $37K/mo)
> "I just looked at all the form builders out there and I thought hey like this is a new product I have one tenth of all the features they offer so I need to be cheaper than all of them"

**John Makavoy** — Mumigo (mobile app, $30K/mo)
> "Being able to set up AB tests for different pay walls to see which one is more effective by trusting the data that you get from these kind of analytics you will have a much better idea of who your customers are and what they want"

**Gil** — Subscribr (SaaS, $30K/mo)
> "A key part of the pre-sale is to have an offer so good that people can't help it but say yes. For the first 10 customers might have not even been profitable but I knew it was something that people would say yes to."

**Saadi** — Super Power Chat GPT (Chrome extension, $20-30K/mo)
> "I didn't ask anyone to pay for any existing features that the extension had and all of those features stayed free and I tried like different pricing like I experimented like higher price lower price"

**Lucas Herman** — Stage Timer (SaaS, $25,000/month)
> "Having a free tier works really well for us. We capture a lot of freelancers that work in this space and they bring it along to the events."

## At $5K-10K/mo

**Jonathan Fishner** — ChartDB (SaaS, $9K/mo)
> "Let usage to let you what to monetize so in the beginning we didn't monetize at all it's just like providing the tool with the utility then like people started to ask to work with their team and then we needed to provide a more complex real-time collaboration"

**Leandro** — Synto Sheets (SaaS, $9K/month)
> "I made a decision to remove the free plan. It was risky. There was a lot of people angry about that. But basically in that moment we went from 5K to 8K in few months."

**Pauline** — AI Crea (SaaS, $8K/mo)
> "If you sell for a low price you have an image of being low cost. By selling higher price you might have less clients but it's less support as well. You have to think about how do you want to be perceived and how much support are you able to deliver."
