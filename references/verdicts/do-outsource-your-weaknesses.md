# DO: Outsource Your Weaknesses Early

> Don't learn everything — hire for what you're bad at.

**27 founders** weighed in on this. **43 total mentions** across 27 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Ryan Chen** — Neuro (e-commerce, $10 million/month)
> "We stuck to our guns and we're like we're just not going to go below this"

**Saba Kenned** — V.io (SaaS, $40M/yr)
> "We took half of our salaries every month religiously put it back into the company account and then used that money to hire two other engineers"

**Ian Meers** — Oceans (agency, $15M/yr)
> "I like to hire from non-traditional places. Looking not totally at like have you done this before but is what you've done before going to set you up for success in this job really really important."

**Lane Wagner** — boot.dev (SaaS, $1M/mo)
> "A lot of founders get way ahead of themselves thinking like I'm going to be the CEO i'm the fucking idea guy right i'm going to delegate everything i'm going to hire that salesperson hire that marketer hire that developer but omitting the hard skills is a big mistake."

**Greg Eisenberg** — Late Checkout (holding company, $10M+/year)
> "I'm not advocating for launch Five products on day one become a solopreneur find a niche build an audience build a community build a product for them all I'm saying is at the end of that once that business is generating at least $100,000 of profit find an operator."

**Blake Anderson** — Riz GPT, Umax, CI (mobile app, $10M total across 3 apps)
> "Hire like five to 10 guys to do an hour or two of work and then stick with the best guy that tends to work very well"

**Tim Stoddard** — Stazzi Internet Marketing (agency, $5.2-5.5M/yr)
> "Read the E-Myth Revisited. I realized that I am in the way of everything. The best thing I can do is document processes, be organized, teach and then get the hell out of the way."

**Ericos** — Kaching Bundles (and Kaching suite of apps) (SaaS, $4.5M/yr)
> "I think finding a co-founder is pretty much similar to doing sales because you have to sell someone an idea that you can make a successful business and you just need their help."

## At $50K-100K/mo

**Alex Lieberman** — Story ARB (agency, $84K/mo)
> "I look for someone who has an obsessive brain I want someone who goes to sleep thinking about the business and I want someone waking up thinking about the business cuz it means they enjoy what they're doing so much."

**Marcos** — The Birdhouse (agency, $70K/mo)
> "After 10 20K a month I'd say every business has kind of a different point where you have to ask yourself do I want to be a solo preneur or do I want to be an entrepreneur"

**Vasco** — Arvo (SaaS, $70K/mo)
> "The wrong way to go about finding creators is going and finding creators because they're very expensive and they have a big ego. What I did was find people who I think can become creators."

**Alex Garcia** — Marketing Exam (and 4 other newsletters) (content site, $65K-70K/mo)
> "Search a very Niche topic on Google and then go on to like Pages two three four like just deep into the archive of Google read some of these people's content that is such a place to find great writers"

**Scott** — None (productized service, $50K/mo)
> "When you start building an audience people will naturally want to work for you because they believe in what you're doing and they think that what you're doing is cool"

## At $10K-50K/mo

**Loic** — Drop Magic (SaaS, $45K/mo)
> "Having a creator in your co-founding team actually has three really good positive impact: derisk the distribution, product insights, and marketing insights."

**Steven Creada** — Puff Count (mobile app, $40K/mo)
> "I recommend only hiring developers from Eastern Europe they're going to give you the best quality code for a cheaper price."

**Aayal and Yali** — PropGPT (mobile app, $30K/mo)
> "Have a co-founder who you will be able to lean on and that will be able to lean on you when things get hard as an entrepreneur."

**Colin Macintosh** — Sheets Resume (SaaS, $20K/mo)
> "You're not going to ever convince some random software engineer to work on something with you that is like your idea without paying them a lot of money nor should they."

**Maddox Schmidtoffer** — DuckMath (content site, $15K/mo)
> "Get someone else who I mentored to actually do the videos so I could save more time doing other things while the person I mentored actually could pump out really quality videos following my format"

## At Unknown

**Thomas** — Empire International (M&A brokerage, over $1.1B in transactions)
> "When it comes to negotiating decide what matters to you the most if you don't know the worst thing to do is just pushing back on every point"

**Hunter Hammett** — Assembly (productized services, multiple seven figures across 4 companies)
> "One of our secrets recruiting is just being like a really great generous employer and that's worked wonders for us."

**Karthik** — Greek House / Athletes Thread (e-commerce, $10M total)
> "We've come into that shop that we work with and said hey we want to work with you these are our prices but we are also going to provide you Tech so that when you service our account it takes one tenth of time to service than it does for your other customers."
