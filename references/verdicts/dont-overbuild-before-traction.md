# DON'T: Overbuild Before You Have Traction

> Building for 6 months without users is the most common founder mistake.

**14 founders** weighed in on this. **15 total mentions** across 14 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Gam Mubes** — lemlist (SaaS, $30M ARR)
> "Pivoted from blue ocean complex tech idea to red ocean with better execution. Why: realized blue ocean was too complex, red ocean means proven product-market fit. Impact: led to successful lemlist launch"

**Lane Wagner** — boot.dev (SaaS, $1M/mo)
> "thinking MVP means shitty product (cost: product would flop). Lesson: MVP should mean minimum quantity not minimum quality"

**Aaron Spivak** — Hush Blankets (e-commerce, $48M in 4 years)
> "Didn't forecast inventory for summer months when weighted blankets are too hot to use (cost: Went from $90K/month to zero sales, nearly shut down business). Lesson: Need to understand seasonal patterns and customer usage before launching"

**Blake Anderson** — Riz GPT, Umax, CI (mobile app, $10M total across 3 apps)
> "First version of Riz GPT was terrible - no notifications, no reviews, bad design, terrible paywall (cost: Initial promos didn't work, only got few downloads). Lesson: You don't need perfect app to start making money, just need MVP and smart marketing strategy"

**Tibo** — Multiple SaaS Portfolio (SaaS, $700K/mo)
> "90% failure rate on products (cost: Years of time if building slowly). Lesson: Build MVPs in days/weeks to fail fast and iterate quickly"

**Thomas Frank** — Ultimate Brain and Creators Companion (digital products, $100K-$120K/mo)
> "Perfectionist tendency - spending weeks on videos and never publishing them (cost: Weeks of work with no output or feedback). Lesson: You have to actually publish and get feedback and go through that cycle"

## At $50K-100K/mo

**Mark Lou** — ShipFast (SaaS, $80K/mo)
> "Spent a year building Tinder for sports lovers without validating or knowing how to monetize (cost: 1 year of time, living with parents making $10/hour). Lesson: Don't build for a year without validation or business plan"

## At $10K-50K/mo

**Cole** — Hide or Die (mobile app, $45K/mo)
> "Spent money on YouTuber hiring platform that failed (cost: Half a million dollars total on two failed projects). Lesson: Build quick and validate rather than over-engineering"

**Steven Creada** — Puff Count (mobile app, $40K/mo)
> "spending months developing random features before launching MVP (cost: months of development time). Lesson: go to market with something simple, get feedback from users, then build on top of it"

**Alejandro and Mario** — Push School (mobile app, $30K/month)
> "Your app needs to have one visually heavy element so try to make your app as visually interesting as you can also your app needs to be super easy to explain if you cannot explain your app in three words it's too complex"

**Connor** — Payout (mobile app, $20K/mo)
> "spent 6-8 months building first social app that failed (cost: 6-8 months of time). Lesson: social apps are super hard to scale as a one-person team, utilities and tools are much easier to scale"

**Ben Boz** — Tech Lockdown (SaaS, $15K/mo)
> "Over complicated landing page builder and burnt out before launching (cost: entire second side project failed). Lesson: didn't think about how to launch it"

**Joseph and Teemo** — Setter AI (SaaS, $10K/mo)
> "Quit job and spent whole year building without traction (cost: 1 year of time). Lesson: Validate before building anything"

## At Unknown

**Pat Walls** — Starter Story (content site, $1M business)
> "Spending months perfecting product before launch without focusing on getting customers (cost: months of time). Lesson: Nobody gives a f about you or the thing that you launched - you need to focus on getting attention first"
