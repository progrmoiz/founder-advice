# DO: Compete on Speed, Not Features

> You can't out-feature incumbents. But you can out-speed and out-care them.

**25 founders** weighed in on this. **32 total mentions** across 25 interviews.

## At $100K+/mo

**Casey Holiday** — Qalo (e-commerce, $30M)
> "A big part of our early strategy at Kayla was the recognition that we were the first mover in the category and that if we didn't go fast somebody else with more money better brand recognition better operators were going to come in and crush us"

**Lane Wagner** — boot.dev (SaaS, $1M/mo)
> "When someone lands on your site as a new entrepreneur it can be really tempting to go look at a bunch of competitors websites and like 'Oh man they're so beautiful they're so well-designed i should make my website look like theirs.' Absolutely you should not do that."

**Colin** — Sheets & Giggles (e-commerce, $1M/month)
> "I have four rules to a good brand name it has to be spell sharable and memorable, it has to connote or denote what you do, you have to have good SEO around it, and if you're going to sell to Americans you got to have a dot com"

**Oliver Brocato** — Tabs Chocolate (e-commerce, $11M/yr)
> "The power of the brand is dropping a product on your website and in 30 seconds it's sold out. The power of the brand is creating a community and like an identity between you and your consumer where they live and breathe and die for your shit."

**Sean** — Alia (SaaS, $4M ARR)
> "If our best customers think of us as a pop-up tool and they pay us for that you can extrapolate and say that a bunch of other customers who think of us as a pop-up tool might pay us some money"

**David Brusser** — FormulaBot (formerly XL FormulaBot) (SaaS, $226,000/mo)
> "My vision moving forward is to build something that cannot be replicated both in chat gbt as well as Microsoft"

**Carl Hughes** — Draft.dev (agency, $2.5M/yr)
> "Go talk to 10 or 12 customers that have tried these other agencies have been unsatisfied why are they unsatisfied what could you do better if you redesign the service from the ground up and that's going to give you a way to differentiate."

**Andre Heckle Jr** — List Kit (SaaS, $200K/mo)
> "The way we built SaaS is the way I think everyone should build a SaaS is find something that's already proven and find out what do people like about them but what don't they like about them where are the bottlenecks."

## At $10K-50K/mo

**Joe** — Weightley (mobile app, $40K/month)
> "We compete a couple different ways one is on price where Weightley has a flat fee of $100 a month to unlock all the features where Yelp and Open Table their flat fees are hundreds of dollars a month."

**Joe Popolis** — AI Book Brand (e-commerce, $390K profit to date)
> "I'm at a point right now where my brand has so much momentum when I see new competitors coming to the space it doesn't scare me it hasn't affected my profit because I have such a loyal customer base"

**Hassam** — Launch Fast (SaaS, $21.8K/mo)
> "Something I learned from Amazon is that branding is everything so I put the branding in and made it feel like a real product"

**Devon** — Supergrow (SaaS, $19,000/month)
> "Deeply understand your competitors use their product day in day out and try to understand what are their core flows try to come up with a new way to solve the core flows."

**Nevo** — Posties (SaaS, $17K/month)
> "You shouldn't be afraid to go open source don't be afraid for somebody copying your product because today the only thing that is winning usually is brand."

**Fernando** — Resume Maker Online and AI Carousels Pro (SaaS, $15K/mo)
> "I'm glad competition exists, it's a good sign. You don't want to be the only one. Keep focus on user feedback instead of trying to one-up competitors."

**Maddox Schmidtoffer** — DuckMath (content site, $15K/mo)
> "I researched and initially copied the best of my competitor's videos from TikTok and just worked two times harder than they did"

**Aayush** — Elephas (SaaS, $150K/year)
> "Know your exact positioning in the market like where do you lie. How does the customer look at the market and where do you fit in."
